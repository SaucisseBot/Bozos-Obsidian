## Encounter Selection

At the start of every fragment, players are presented with two possible bosses.

```text
Fragment Start

Boss A
Boss B
```

Each boss contains three possible Affix Pairs.

An Affix Pair combines:

* One positive modifier
* One negative modifier

Players first choose their boss.

They then choose the Affix Pair they wish to play.

```text
Choose Boss
      ↓
Choose Affix Pair
      ↓
Play Fragment
```

This creates meaningful decisions while allowing players to shape the type of challenge they want to experience.

## Affix Design

Affixes exist to create variety rather than numerical optimization.

Positive affixes should introduce fun mechanics, unexpected situations, and unique opportunities.

Examples:

* Healing rain periodically falls across the area.
* Treasure goblins may appear.
* Defeated enemies release chain lightning.
* A mysterious merchant can appear before the boss.
* Elite enemies grant temporary blessings.

Negative affixes should introduce additional mechanics and constraints rather than simply increasing enemy statistics.

Examples:

* Enemies explode upon death.
* Additional enemy packs appear.
* Players periodically leave dangerous zones behind them.
* Environmental hazards become active.
* Boss abilities occur more frequently.
